RUO: Music Player

Designed in a group for an Arduino and electronics class, RUO is an interactive sound system and decorative statement piece built to bring physicality back to music listening. Its defining feature is a rotating magnetic interface: twisting the bottom-right cylinder uses internal magnets and cams to rotate the screen into view and power the device on. The remaining controls are wired to a hidden Arduino that reads local audio files and communicates with Processing to handle playback.

Colorado Symphony Poster

Project parameters: Design a 10"×16" folded mailer poster for the Colorado Symphony that works as both an expressive, abstract poster on one side and a clear, structured season schedule on the other, using only two typefaces, three spot colors, geometric elements, and no photos. I went with an abstract Colorado sunrise over the mountains, letting the geometry and limited color palette do the heavy lifting.

Boulder Hike Finder

Designed for Computational Foundations 2 final project implementing binary search tree algorithms: Boulder is one of the premiere cities for hiking, with an estimated 170+ hikes in the greater county area alone, making an impressive 300+ miles of trails to explore. For people looking to hike, that number can be overwhelming. Boulder Hike Finder implements Binary Search Trees to rank different attributes, such as elevation, distance, and rating, to help users find the perfect hike fit for them.

Bad Volume Design

Web design final, a design challenge requiring the creation of an intentionally frustrating user volume control interface built in Java. My design requires users drag and drop post it to cover a speaker, leaning into the most tedious interaction I could think of. The volume is calculated by measuring how much each square overlaps the speaker, using randomly sized squares to add to the difficulty.

Boulder Problems

This collection features many faces of one subject: climbing. All shot on location in Boulder, Colorado, bouldering, sport climbing, and high-class scrambling are on display, along with colorful landscapes and rock formations, as the views and the activities go hand in hand. A grainy effect is present in nearly all the photos, inspired by the early 2000s camera used to capture these photos, and later enhanced in editing to give an old-school REI magazine or Polaroid feel, which also frames a handful of the climbers presented. Designed for a photography midterm to be displayed as a picture book.
